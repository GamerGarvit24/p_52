Origami is an ancient art form that is believed to have been around for nearly 2000 years, derived from the Japanese tradition of paper-folding

Facts About Origami:

    Origami is derived from two Japanese words:Ori (folded) and Kami (paper).
    The first book ever published about Origami was entitled "Sembazuru Orikata"(Thousand Crane Folding)
    As a good luck token for all the future dangers to be faced, Samurais commonly used to present gifts of origami to each other



1. Pigeon Origami

Interesting facts about Pigeons:

  • Pigeons are very complex and intelligent animals.
  • They are one of a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’
  • They can also recognise each letter of the human alphabet and differentiate between photos.
  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They are often seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.

2. Chameleon Origami

Interesting facts about chameleons:

  • Almost half of the world’s chameleon species live on the island of Madagascar.
  • They mailny change color in order to communicate or regulate bodt temperature.
  • Chameleon eyes have a 360-degree arc of vision and can see two directions at once.

3. Cicada Origami

Interesting facts about Cicadas:

  • The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar.
  • Cicadas make noise like toy frogs, especially male Cicadas
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.

4. Camel Origami

Interesting facts about camels:

  • The word ‘camel’ comes from an Arabic word, which means beauty
  • There are more than 14 million camels in the world
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

5. Panda Origami

Interesting facts about Pandas:

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • A 45-kilo adult panda can eat 12 to 38 kilos of bamboo a day.
  • Pandas need at least 2 different bamboo species in their range to avoid starvation

6. Frog Origami

Interesting facts about Frog:

  • There are over 5,000 species of frog.
  • Frogs don’t need to drink water as they absorb it through their skin.
  • A frog’s call is unique to its species, and some frog calls can be heard up to a mile away.

7. Dog Origami

Interesting facts about Dog:

  • Dogs noses are wet to help absorb scent chemicals
  • Newfoundlands are amazing lifeguards
  • A Bloodhound’s sense of smell can be used as evidence in court

8. Bear Origami

Interesting facts about Bear :

  • Bears have a large brain and are one of the more intelligent mammals.
  • A bear typically lives up to 25 years in the wild and 50 years in captivity.
  • During winter, bears hibernate up to 100 days.
